ABOUT US

The Rowan University Rocketry Team participates in the annual International Rocket Engineering Competition, the world's largest intercollegiate rocket engineering competition. With over 150 schools from 22 countries, this event brings together thousands of students, faculty, and industry professionals. Our mission this year is to build a sounding rocket that will exceed 10,000 feet in altitude to compete in the 2025 competition.

Our team of 20 interdisciplinary engineering students is divided into six sub-teams: airframe, avionics, payload, recovery, propulsion, and fins. Each sub-team is integral to the rocket's development, from structural design to data transmission and recovery.

This project not only pushes technical boundaries but also fosters teamwork, problem-solving, and professional development, preparing us for careers in aerospace engineering.

SUB-SYSTEMS

RECOVERY

The Recovery Sub-team is responsible for managing rocket separations and deploying two parachutes, using a compressed air system with black powder as a backup for redundancy. They follow competition guidelines and require skills in R&D, 3D modeling, machining, and testing to ensure reliable and safe recovery.

AVIONICS

The Avionics sub-team is responsible for the electronics contained within the rocket. It is composed of a three board structure regulating power, radio controls, and a recovery system. This system is divided into split parts handling two different types of separation including compressed air and black powder.

AIRFRAME

The airframe consists of 5 components: the fiberglass nosecone, main body blue-tube, sustainer blue-tube, fiberglass couplers, and bulkheads. These components ensure that the rocket maintains structural integrity, minimizes drag for optimal flight performance, and securely houses the sub-systems.

PROPULSION

The Propulsion Sub-team is responsible for selecting and integrating the rocket's motor to ensure it achieves the desired height and maintains a stable, safe flight. They use flight simulations and 3D modeling to meet thrust-to-weight ratio requirements and design a precise, rigid motor mount.

PAYLOAD

The Payload Sub-team is responsible for designing, building, and testing a functional payload that includes an Arduino-powered rover, capable of autonomously detecting landing and performing tasks like environmental exploration and video capture.

FINS

The Fins Sub-team is responsible for designing, constructing, and testing rocket fins to ensure stability during flight and prevent flutter. They focus on selecting appropriate size, shape, and material composition to maintain the center of pressure below the center of gravity and to ensure the fins do not break upon landing.

WHY SPONSOR?

Over the course of two semesters, our team dedicates itself to designing, building, and testing a rocket. These efforts range from sourcing parts, CAD preparation for competing at the prestigious Spaceport America Cup.

This ambitious project requires financial support without the aid of university sponsors, where contributions play a crucial role in turning this vision into reality. The generosity of our sponsors enables us to compete at the highest level.

What sponsorship will provide to us?

  • Funding for Critical Components: Your support will mean accessing costly essential parts of the rocket, enabling us to build a cutting-edge, high-performance craft that meets the rigorous demands of competition.
  • Professional Development: The sponsorship provides invaluable opportunities for our dedicated team members. This funding allows them to attend the Spaceport America Cup, presenting their engineering solutions to industry professionals.
  • Educational Impact: Your contribution directly supports hands-on learning, facilitating a comprehensive educational experience that equips our students with practical engineering knowledge as they prepare for successful careers in aerospace engineering.

What sponsorship will provide to you?

  • Brand Exposure and Positive Association: Align your company with a leading team and represent your brand to a broad audience.
  • Access to Emerging Talent: Connect with motivated engineering students who demonstrate exceptional technical abilities.
  • Community Support: Help develop a dynamic and ambitious team.
  • Marketing Opportunities: Your logo will appear on our team's website and social media platforms.
  • Network Growth: Engage with professionals throughout the aerospace industry, creating valuable business relationships.

PAST ROCKET

Rowan Rocketry made history last year with the successful launch at nationals, at the 2023 Spaceport America Cup. Competing against 75 teams, we proudly placed first and achieved a major milestone. Our rocket soared to new heights, breaking all team records and proving our talent and skill, yet having all the lessons learned and getting us just to succeed, but to win, at the 2024 launch.
